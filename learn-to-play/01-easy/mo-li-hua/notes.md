# Mo Li Hua (茉莉花 / Jasmine Flower)

**Tradition:** Trad Chinese folk song (pre-1900); popularised by opera and international recordings
**Public domain:** Yes — traditional folk melody, collected in various sources before 1900
**Arrangement:** G major pentatonic for D xiao. Range G4–E5 (2nd octave of the xiao).

## Range check
Pitches G4–E5 sit in the xiao's comfortable second octave. No overblowing required at this range.

## Practice tip
This is one of the most-recorded Chinese folk songs in the world. Take it slow —
the xiao's tone needs room. Each phrase is a long breath arc; don't rush the quarter notes.
