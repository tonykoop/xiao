# Jig: Headstock-Driven Bore — Tailstock Square-Stock Carrier

**Project:** XIAO-001 · G Nan Xiao  
**Operation:** OP-02, OP-03  
**Decision basis:** jig-decision.md — Decision 1

---

## Purpose

Hold the xiao blank stationary (non-rotating) in the tailstock position while the lathe headstock spins the long drill bit. The tailstock quill advances the blank into the spinning bit. See `references/techniques/headstock-driven-deep-bore-drilling.md` for full technique details.

## Materials

- (2×) 3/4 in Baltic birch plywood or MDF, 12 in × 6 in
- (4×) 1/4 in carriage bolts + wing nuts (for laminating and clamping)
- (2×) toggle clamps (De-Sta-Co 225-U or equivalent) — anti-rotation
- (1×) Lathe tool-rest base or flat sled that rides the lathe bed ways

## Fabrication

1. Cut two plywood panels, laminate to 1.5 in total thickness.
2. Mark the center of the panel face. Locate spindle-axis height (measure from lathe bed to spindle centerline).
3. Cut a square pocket centered at spindle-axis height: 1.5 in × 1.5 in + 0.003 in slip clearance.
4. Mount toggle clamps on left and right faces of the pocket, positioned to engage the blank faces.
5. Attach the assembly to a sled that slides along the lathe bed ways without rocking.
6. Verify alignment: insert a straight rod through the pocket; the rod should run parallel to spindle axis within 0.010 in over 12 in.

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] Carrier mounted, sled verified square to lathe bed
- [ ] Spindle-axis alignment checked with dial indicator (center of pocket within 0.010 in of spindle axis)
- [ ] Toggle clamps open and ready
- [ ] Scrap blank center-punched and inserted in carrier
- [ ] Anti-rotation clamps engaged — verify blank cannot rotate
- [ ] Pilot bit mounted in headstock chuck; runout < 0.005 in at tip
- [ ] Depth stop (tape flag) set at 25 in from chuck face
